City of Calgary UX Redesign

Role: UX Researcher & Designer â€¢ Tools: Figma â€¢ Year: 2024

Problem

The City of Calgary website is a large information hub used by diverse audiencesâ€”residents, students, job seekers, and newcomers. During initial reviews, users described the navigation as confusing, overcrowded, and difficult to scan. Essential tasks such as accessing city services, permits, and transit information required unnecessary searching and backtracking.

Research & Insights

To understand where friction occurred, I conducted interviews and ran a short usability survey. The feedback revealed three key issues:

  • Overwhelming navigation: too many competing options on the homepage.
  • Lack of clear hierarchy: important services were buried several layers deep.
  • Inconsistent layout patterns: users could not predict where information would appear.

Process

  1. Information Architecture Redesign: grouped services by user intent and simplified top-level categories to reduce cognitive load.
  2. Task Flow Mapping: rebuilt primary journeys such as paying a bill, applying for a permit, and viewing service updates.
  3. Wireframes & Mid-Fi Screens: created a cleaner homepage structure with clearer hierarchy, improved spacing, and predictable navigation.
  4. Design System Foundations: built a lightweight token-based system for colours, typography, spacing, and reusable components.

Outcome

The redesigned structure significantly improved clarity and usability during testing. Users completed assigned tasks with fewer clicks and reported higher confidence navigating the site. The new IA and consistent UI components reduced friction, created predictable patterns, and made key city services more accessible.

What I Learned

This project strengthened my ability to translate qualitative feedback into structured UX decisions. By focusing on IA, hierarchy, and consistency, I learned how even small improvements in organization can dramatically impact user success and satisfaction.
